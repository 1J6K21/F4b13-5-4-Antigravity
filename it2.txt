If these files are supposed to represent the final public-facing research output, I'd be careful.

They're **good as mockups**, but right now they read more like a pitch deck than research.

A skeptical AI researcher, founder, or investor is immediately going to ask:

> Where did the numbers come from?

For example:

* "+11 pts User Preference"
* "310% Increase in Insights"
* "4x Actionability"

appear as hard results in both documents, but I don't see any methodology explaining how those metrics were measured.  

If those are placeholders, label them as placeholders.

---

### What I would change

#### Replace certainty with investigation

Current:

> Behavioral scaffolding alone accounts for a massive leap in perceived intelligence. 

Stronger:

> Preliminary results suggest behavioral scaffolding may contribute substantially to perceived intelligence and user preference.

This sounds more credible.

---

#### Avoid "leaked Claude prompt" as the centerpiece

Current deck:

> We ported the leaked Claude Fable 5 prompt... 

I'd instead say:

> We analyzed a publicly circulating Claude Fable system prompt and extracted its behavioral scaffolding.

That sounds like research.

---

#### Add a missing slide

The biggest omission is:

### "What Actually Transferred?"

A table like:

| Capability         | Transferred? |
| ------------------ | ------------ |
| Tone               | High         |
| Structure          | High         |
| Tool Usage Habits  | Medium       |
| Strategic Thinking | Medium       |
| Raw Reasoning      | Low          |
| Knowledge          | None         |

That's the finding people actually care about.

---

#### Add a surprising failure

Real research has failures.

Example:

> Compression preserved safety behavior but degraded conversational naturalness.

or

> Founder Mode improved startup analysis but hurt neutral information retrieval.

People trust research more when it includes negative findings.

---

### The biggest opportunity

Right now the deck's thesis is:

> System Prompt = OS

That's good.

The stronger thesis is:

> Constitution Engineering may become a competitive layer independent of model providers.

In other words:

* OpenAI builds hardware.
* Anthropic builds hardware.
* Google builds hardware.

But:

* Startups build constitutions.

That's a venture-scale idea.
