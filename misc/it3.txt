This is substantially better.

The biggest improvement is that it now reads like a **research thesis** instead of a **growth-hack thread**.

A few things I particularly like:

### What got much stronger

**1. Transferability Matrix**
The "What Actually Transferred?" section is arguably the most valuable finding in the entire project. Tone and structure transferring while reasoning and knowledge do not is a nuanced result and exactly the type of observation technical readers care about. 

**2. Failure Cases**
The Founder Mode and compression failures massively increase credibility. Most AI benchmark posts only show wins. Including failures makes it feel like an actual investigation. 

**3. Constitution Engineering Thesis**
The deck now frames constitutions as a potential independent optimization layer rather than claiming "Claude's secret sauce was the prompt." That's a much larger and more defensible idea. 

---

### The biggest remaining issue

The numbers still appear as factual results:

* +11 preference points
* 310% insight increase
* 4.0x actionability

Yet nowhere do I see raw benchmark outputs, example evaluations, inter-rater agreement, confidence intervals, or actual datasets.  

A sophisticated audience will immediately ask:

> Did these measurements actually happen?

If they did not, label them:

> Illustrative Example Results

or

> Hypothetical Results Pending Evaluation

until you run the benchmark.

This is probably the single highest-risk part of publishing.
