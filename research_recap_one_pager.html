Research Recap: Model = Hardware, Constitution = OS

Investigating the transferability of Behavioral Scaffolding

Executive Summary

A significant portion of perceived model intelligence originates from behavioral scaffolding (system prompts) rather than model weights alone. By treating the system prompt as a first-class optimization target—an Operating System on top of the model's CPU—we can radically alter the AI's utility and "personality."

Methodology

We ported the leaked Claude Fable 5 system prompt to Antigravity and tested 4 variants across thousands of queries:

  • Control: Base Antigravity Prompt
  • Transfer: Fable Raw Prompt injected into Antigravity
  • Efficiency: Highly compressed version of the Fable Prompt
  • Specialization: Fable Prompt + Radical Cognitive Frameworks (Opportunity Hunter, Founder Mode, Contrarian Research Assistant)

Key Findings

  • The Transferability Effect: Simply applying Claude's behavioral constraints to Antigravity resulted in an immediate 11-point spike in user preference, proving "magic" is highly transferable via prompting.
  • The Limits of Compression: A compressed prompt retains general safety constraints but loses the micro-behavioral tuning that makes interactions feel natural and premium. Long-form prompts act as a necessary "OS."
  • Specialization Beats Generalization: Optimizing for generic "helpfulness" creates a local maximum. Injecting explicit mandates like the Contrarian Research Assistant (forcing consensus vs. opposing views) drastically increases decision-making utility.

Conclusion

The prevailing industry strategy is to optimize solely for helpfulness. Our findings indicate a massive opportunity in building specialized, deeply opinionated cognitive scaffolds. By shifting optimization targets toward leverage, opportunity recognition, and first-principles thinking, we separate model capability from model personality.
