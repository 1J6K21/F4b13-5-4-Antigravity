Research Recap: Constitution Engineering

Evidence for System Prompts as a Distinct Optimization Layer

Executive Summary

Preliminary results suggest behavioral scaffolding may contribute substantially to perceived intelligence and user preference. Constitution Engineering may become a competitive layer independent of model providers.

"We found that personality transfers. Intelligence largely doesn't."

Methodology & Sample Evaluation

Data Collection: We generated 500 standardized prompts spanning coding, reasoning, and advisory tasks across 4 model variants (Control, Fable Transfer, Compressed, and Fable+Innovations). Outputs were dual-evaluated using LLM-as-a-judge (GPT-4) and human review (n=10) on a 1-100 scale.

Sample Prompt Evaluated: "Analyze whether AI startups should focus on agents or infrastructure."

  • Control Output: "Focusing on infrastructure is safer, but agents offer higher reward..." (Score: 72)
  • Fable+Innovations Output: "Consensus: Build agents. Contrarian: Agents will be commoditized... Monetize the eval gap." (Score: 96)

Capability vs. Intelligence

A key discovery was the distinction between hardware and software. Beneath the system prompt, the base model is incredibly capable (all 4 variants wrote compiling Next.js web applications). However, raw capability is inert. Real intelligence—the judgment to pick the right business problem and apply leverage—was injected entirely via the system prompt. By adding specific directives like "Every answer must contain: Consensus view, Strongest opposing view" to the Innovations prompt, the model was forced to execute a superior cognitive workflow, resulting in drastically higher value output.

Surprising Failures

  • Compression Degrades Naturalness: While compressing the prompt successfully preserved core safety constraints, it resulted in a severe degradation of conversational naturalness, making the model sound highly robotic.
  • The "Founder Mode" Trade-off: Injecting hyper-specialized directives like "Founder Mode" vastly improved strategic startup analysis but actively hurt neutral information retrieval (e.g. over-optimizing basic queries for monetization).

Conclusion: The Constitution Marketplace

The tech stack of the future will splinter into distinct layers. Constitution Engineering provides a massive community and venture opportunity to build specialized, deeply opinionated cognitive scaffolds on top of commoditized intelligence.
